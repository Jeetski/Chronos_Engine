# How Chronos Is Structured

Chronos organizes life as a hierarchy of nested layers.

![Hierarchy of Chronos layers](/assets/images/guides/Hierarchy.png)

At a high level, it looks like this:

- `Week`
- `Day`
- `Routine`
- `Subroutine`
- `Microroutine`
- `Item`

You can read this from outside to inside.

A `Week` holds your days. A `Day` holds the parts of that day. A `Routine` holds a recurring area of activity. A `Subroutine` breaks that routine into a smaller chunk. A `Microroutine` breaks it down even further. An `Item` is the smallest unit: one concrete thing.

This matters because life is layered.

You do not just "do tasks." You move through structures:
- a week contains a day
- a day contains routines
- a routine contains repeatable steps
- those steps contain specific actions

Chronos gives those layers a visible form.
